Blue whale

  • Goldbogen and his colleagues report their findings about how blue whales change their foraging behavior depending on prey density: Prey Distribution and Foraging Strategies.
  • Dr. David E. Cade and Prof. Goldbogen with their group also developed bio-logging devices equipped with inertial measurement units — particularly accelerometers, magnetometers, and pressure sensors — which have revolutionized our ability to study animals and led to many fascinating research on whales beyond.

Giants of the field

  • Prof. Ruth Gates.
    Her research was dedicated to understanding coral reef ecosystems, specifically coral-algal symbiosis and the capacity for corals to acclimatize under future climate change conditions.
  • Prof. James Estes with "Trophic cascades" and Prof. Robert T. Paine with his breakthrough work "Food Web Complexity and Species Diversity".
    They are the pioneers discovering about the organization of marine communities, much of their research on keystone species concept.
  • Dr. Bryan Frazier and his colleagues are doing many amazing research on sharks.
  • Dr. John S. Sparks' research focuses on the evolution and function of marine bioluminescence (the ability of living things to produce their own light) and biofluorescence (the ability of living things to reemit light in different wavelengths).
